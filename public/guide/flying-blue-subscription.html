Short answer: Subscribe to Miles is for buying miles cheaply (no XP). Flying Blue Extra is for perks and status acceleration (no miles included). Both have narrow use cases. Most travelers should skip Flying Blue Extra entirely.

Two Different Products, Two Different Purposes

Flying Blue offers two subscription products that serve completely different needs. Subscribe to Miles lets you buy miles monthly at a discount. Flying Blue Extra (launched November 2025) gives perks like bonus XP and exclusive Promo Rewards. This guide provides an honest, critical analysis of both.

Subscribe to Miles: Key Details

  • Cancel anytime (withdrawal right within 14 working days, see official terms)

Flying Blue Extra: Premium Annual Subscription

The Honest Verdict

Subscribe to Miles: Cautious Recommendation

Verdict: Useful for short-term, tactical purchases.

Subscribe for one or two months when you need to top off for a specific redemption, then cancel. At ‚Ç¨0.0125/mile (Complete tier), it is cheaper than promo purchases but more expensive than transfer bonuses. Do not subscribe speculatively.

Flying Blue Extra Essential (‚Ç¨379): Not Recommended for Most

Verdict: Too expensive for the benefits provided.

The Exclusive Promo Rewards are the main draw, but they vary wildly each month. Unless you consistently find value in them, you are paying ‚Ç¨379 for 2 lounge passes and minor discounts. Most travelers will not break even.

Flying Blue Extra Extended (‚Ç¨699): Niche Product

Verdict: Only for status chasers without existing elite benefits.

The 20% XP bonus is meaningful if you fly frequently on AF/KLM and are pushing toward Silver or Gold. But if you already have Gold or higher, the lounge passes are worthless and the XP bonus provides diminishing returns. The reward ticket discount is so restricted it is nearly unusable.

üí° My Take: Flying Blue Extra feels like a product designed to extract revenue from members rather than provide genuine value. The restrictions on the 10% reward discount (no stopovers, no Promo Rewards, max 2 bookings) show that AF/KLM wants your subscription money but does not want you to actually use the benefits effectively. Subscribe to Miles is more honest: you pay, you get miles, no games.

FAQ

What is the difference between Subscribe to Miles and Flying Blue Extra?

Subscribe to Miles is a monthly subscription to purchase miles at a discount (1.34 cents per mile at the highest tier). Flying Blue Extra is an annual subscription (‚Ç¨379 to ‚Ç¨699) that provides benefits like bonus XP, exclusive Promo Rewards access, and lounge passes. They serve different purposes: one for acquiring miles cheaply, the other for accelerating status and getting perks.

Is Flying Blue Extra worth ‚Ç¨379 or ‚Ç¨699 per year?

For most travelers, Flying Blue Extra is not worth it. The Essential tier (‚Ç¨379) offers limited value unless you can use multiple Exclusive Promo Rewards. The Extended tier (‚Ç¨699) is only worthwhile if you fly frequently on AF or KLM and need the XP boost to reach status. Existing elite members with lounge access get minimal benefit.

Does Subscribe to Miles give XP?

No. Subscribe to Miles only provides award miles for redemptions. It does not provide any XP toward status qualification. Flying Blue Extra Extended provides bonus XP, and only on eligible paid flights, not on purchased miles.
