What is Flying Blue UXP? Ultimate XP Explained

Last updated: December 2025 ¬∑ 7 min read ¬∑ Based on official Flying Blue program rules

R
By Remco Flying Blue Platinum ¬∑ 20 years in the program

Definition

UXP (Ultimate XP) is a special type of experience points that only counts toward Flying Blue Ultimate status. Unlike regular XP (earned on all SkyTeam flights and partners), UXP is only earned on flights operated by Air France or KLM. You need 900 UXP while maintaining Platinum status (300 XP) to reach Ultimate. Partner flights and credit cards earn XP but zero UXP.

Ultimate Status Requirements

To reach Ultimate status, you must meet both requirements simultaneously:

  1. Be Platinum: Have earned 300 XP in your qualification year
  2. Earn 900 UXP: During the same qualification year, on AF/KL operated flights

Key insight: UXP is a subset of XP. Every UXP point also counts as regular XP, but not every XP point counts as UXP.

üìä Scenario Example: You fly 10 long-haul round trips earning 100 XP each = 1,000 XP total. If 7 trips were on KLM and 3 were on Delta codeshares operated by Delta, you'd have 1,000 XP but only 700 UXP. You'd be Platinum but not Ultimate.

UXP Rollover Rules

Like regular XP, UXP can roll over to your next qualification year:

  • Maximum UXP rollover: 900 UXP
  • Total cap: You can have at most 1,800 UXP in a year (900 earned + 900 rollover)
  • UXP rollover is separate from XP rollover
üìä Rollover Example: You're Ultimate and earned 400 XP and 1,200 UXP this year.
‚Ä¢ XP rollover: 400 - 300 = 100 XP (under 300 cap, all rolls over)
‚Ä¢ UXP rollover: 1,200 - 900 = 300 excess. Less than 900 cap, so 300 rolls over.
‚Ä¢ Next year starts with 100 XP and 300 UXP already banked

Is Pursuing UXP Worth It?

Ultimate Makes Sense If:

  • You naturally fly 80%+ on Air France or KLM anyway
  • Platinum for Life (10 consecutive years) is your goal
  • You'd reach 900 UXP without expensive rerouting
  • Premium lounge access matters to you

Stay at Platinum If:

  • You fly significant mileage on Delta or other partners
  • Rerouting to AF/KL would cost significantly more
  • You rely on Amex XP to maintain status
  • The incremental benefits don't justify the effort
üí° Strategic Insight: Calculate your natural UXP earning before pursuing Ultimate. If you'd naturally earn 600 UXP and need 900, you need an extra 300 UXP. That is roughly 3-4 additional long-haul AF/KL round trips. Is that ‚Ç¨2,000-4,000 worth the incremental Ultimate benefits?
