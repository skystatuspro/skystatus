What is a Mileage Run?

Last updated: January 2025 ¬∑ 8 min read

Definition

A mileage run (also called a "status run" or "XP run" in Flying Blue) is a flight taken primarily to earn airline status points rather than to reach a destination. The traveler books flights specifically to accumulate XP toward elite status thresholds, prioritizing cost per XP over convenience or destination preference.

Why People Do Mileage Runs

Mileage runs exist because airline elite status provides tangible benefits that can exceed the cost of extra flights:

Types of Mileage Runs

1. Day Trip Runs

Fly out and back the same day. Works best for short-haul destinations with multiple daily frequencies.

  • Pros: No hotel needed, minimal time investment
  • Cons: Limited to nearby destinations, tiring

2. Overnight Runs

Stay one night at destination. Allows longer routes with better XP earning.

  • Pros: Access to longer/better routes, can explore destination
  • Cons: Hotel cost, more time investment

3. Positioning Runs

Fly to another city to catch a cheaper onward fare. Common when origin airport has expensive fares.

4. Business Class Runs

Book Business Class on routes where the price premium is low relative to XP gain. Sometimes short-haul Business is only ‚Ç¨50‚Äì100 more than Economy but earns 2‚Äì3x XP.
