What is an XP Run? (The Flying Blue Mileage Run)

The Flying Blue Difference

In the aviation world, people talk about "Mileage Runs". But in Flying Blue, miles don't determine your status. XP (Experience Points) do. Therefore, a Flying Blue member does an XP Run. The goal isn't to fly far, but to earn the maximum number of XP for the lowest price. This often means flying shorter distances with more connections rather than one long direct flight.

The Golden Rule: More Legs = More XP

This is the secret to a successful XP run. Flying Blue awards XP per flight segment (leg).

If you fly direct from A to B, you get XP once. If you fly A to Hub to B, you get XP twice. Since connecting flights are often priced similarly (or cheaper) than direct flights, adding a stopover is the easiest way to boost your status.

Types of XP Runs

1. The "Hop" (Short-Haul Business)

Flying Business Class within Europe is often the most efficient method. A short flight (e.g. AMS-London) in Business earns 15 XP. A round trip is 30 XP. Do this via a hub (e.g. AMS-Paris-Nice) and a Business round trip yields 60 XP.

2. The "Long Weekend"

Fly Friday evening, return Sunday. Choose a destination in the "Long 1" band (like Dubai or New York) but with a connection. Economy earns ~40 XP return (connected), Business earns ~90-120 XP return (connected).

3. Positioning Runs

If flights from your home airport are expensive, fly to a secondary airport (e.g. Dusseldorf, Brussels, Billund) to start your main journey. This adds legs (XP) and often lowers the fare significantly.

üí° Pro Tip: Short-haul Business Class is the XP runner's best friend. The price premium over Economy is often small, but the XP earning is 3x higher (15 XP vs 5 XP per leg).

When an XP Run Makes Sense

‚úÖ Do an XP Run If:

  • You are close to a threshold (e.g., 165 XP toward Gold's 180)
  • Your qualification year ends soon and you will lose progress
  • You value lounge access highly for your upcoming travel
  • You can find a "sweet spot" route (High XP, Low Cost)

‚ùå Skip the XP Run If:

  • You are far from the next level (e.g. 50 XP)
  • You only fly once or twice a year (benefits won't pay off)
  • You have to book expensive last-minute fares
  • The time cost outweighs the status benefits

Finding Good Deals

Timing

  • Book 3-6 weeks out: Sweet spot for pricing
  • Fly Tuesday-Thursday: Cheapest days, empty flights
  • Watch for sales: Flying Blue and KLM Real Deal Days

Route Selection

  • Always Connect: Direct flights are XP killers. Connect via CDG (Air France) or AMS (KLM).
  • Check Neighbors: Check flights from BRU, DUS, or LUX instead of AMS/CDG.
