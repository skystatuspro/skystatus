Strategy 3: The Positioning Flight Strategy

Timeline: Variable | Cost: ‚Ç¨1,800-2,500 | Most efficient

Direct flights from hubs (AMS/CDG) earn fewer XP than connecting flights. Adding a segment increases your XP significantly because XP is calculated per leg.

Direct vs Connected:

  • Direct: AMS ‚Üí New York (Business) = 30 XP one way
  • Connected: AMS ‚Üí Paris ‚Üí New York (Business) = 15 XP + 30 XP = 45 XP one way

By simply adding a stopover in Paris (for Dutch flyers) or Amsterdam (for French flyers), you earn 50% more XP for roughly the same price.

XP Accelerators

The Flying Blue co-branded AMEX cards can contribute bonus XP annually:

  • Silver Card: 15 XP annually
  • Gold Card: 30 XP annually
  • Platinum Card: 60 XP annually
  • These stack with flight earnings and count toward status

Double XP Promotions

Flying Blue occasionally runs promotions. Sign up for Flying Blue emails to catch these:

  • Double XP on specific routes
  • Bonus XP for sustainable fuel (SAF) contributions
üí° Pro Tip: There is no status bonus for XP earnings. Silver members earn the exact same XP for a flight as Explorer members. The "bonus" you get with status applies only to Miles (spendable currency), not XP (status currency).

Booking Tips for Maximum XP

Fare Class Matters

Not all Economy tickets are equal. While XP is generally based on cabin and distance, some specific deep-discount fares (rare) might have restrictions. Standard Economy and Flex fares are safe bets.

Book Direct

Always book directly through Air France, KLM, or FlyingBlue.com to ensure:

  • XP credits correctly to your account
  • Status benefits are applied
  • You can easily see the estimated XP before booking
