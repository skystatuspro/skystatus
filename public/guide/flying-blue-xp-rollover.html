Flying Blue XP Rollover: How It Works

Last updated: January 2025 ¬∑ 6 min read ¬∑ Based on official Flying Blue program rules

Definition

XP Rollover is a Flying Blue feature that lets excess XP carry forward to your next qualification year. When you earn more XP than needed to maintain your status, the surplus (up to 300 XP maximum) rolls over, giving you a head start on the following year. For Ultimate members, up to 900 UXP can also roll over separately.

The Rollover Formula

Rollover XP = min(XP Earned - Status Threshold, 300)

In plain terms: your rollover equals your total XP minus what you needed, capped at 300 XP.

Rollover Caps by Status

Status XP Threshold Max XP Rollover Max UXP Rollover
Silver 100 XP 300 XP N/A
Gold 180 XP 300 XP N/A
Platinum 300 XP 300 XP N/A
Ultimate 300 XP + 900 UXP 300 XP 900 UXP

When Rollover Happens

Scenario 1: Normal Requalification

Your 12-month cycle ends, you've met the XP threshold, and you keep your status. Rollover is automatically calculated and applied to your new cycle.

Scenario 2: Level-Up

You earn enough XP mid-cycle to reach the next status. Your cycle immediately resets, and excess XP from that moment rolls over.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Timing Note: When you level up, only XP earned up to and including that moment counts. If you hit Gold on June 15th with 195 XP, your rollover is 15 XP‚Äîeven if you earn 50 more XP on June 16th. Those 50 XP count toward your new cycle, not as additional rollover.

Scenario 3: Status Drop (No Rollover)

If you don't earn enough XP to maintain status, you drop one level. There is no rollover when you drop status. Your new cycle starts at 0 XP in the lower tier.

UXP Rollover for Ultimate Members

Ultimate members have separate UXP rollover rules:

  • Maximum UXP rollover: 900 UXP
  • Total cap: You can have at most 1,800 UXP in a year (900 earned + 900 rollover)
  • UXP rollover operates independently of XP rollover

üìä Example: Ultimate Member Rollover

Situation: You earned 400 XP and 1,200 UXP this year.

XP rollover: 400 - 300 = 100 XP (under 300 cap, all rolls over)

UXP rollover: 1,200 - 900 = 300 UXP excess, but max is 900, so 900 rolls over

Result: Next year starts with 100 XP and 900 UXP

Strategies to Maximize Rollover Value

Strategy 1: Time Large Trips

If you're approaching the 300 XP rollover cap near the end of your qualification year, consider delaying a large trip until after your cycle resets. Those XP will count toward next year instead of being capped.

Strategy 2: Know Your Cycle Date

Many members don't realize their qualification year doesn't follow the calendar year‚Äîit's 12 months from when you first achieved your current status. Track this date to optimize timing.

Strategy 3: Consider Level-Up Timing

If you're Silver with 170 XP in month 10, consider whether to:

  • Push for Gold now (starting a new 12-month Gold cycle with minimal rollover)
  • Wait until after your Silver requalification (keeping accumulated XP as larger rollover)

Common Questions

Does rollover XP count toward the next status level?

Yes. If you start your Gold year with 50 XP rollover, you only need 250 more XP to reach Platinum (300 total required).

Can I lose rollover XP?

Yes, in two scenarios: (1) If you drop status, there's no rollover. (2) If you exceed 300 XP rollover, the excess is lost.

Is there any way to increase the 300 cap?

No. The 300 XP cap is fixed and applies to all status levels. Ultimate members get separate UXP rollover (up to 900) but the XP cap remains 300.
