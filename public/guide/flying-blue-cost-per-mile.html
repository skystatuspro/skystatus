CPM vs Value Per Mile

Do not confuse these two metrics. They measure opposite sides of the equation.

  • CPM (Cost Per Mile): What you PAID to acquire the mile (lower is better)
  • Value Per Mile: What the mile is WORTH when you redeem it (higher is better)

Your goal is simple: Low CPM + High Value Per Mile = Profit. The spread between them determines whether you come out ahead or behind.

üìä Profit Example

Acquire miles via transfer bonus at ‚Ç¨0.008 CPM

Redeem for Business Class at ‚Ç¨0.025 value

Profit = ‚Ç¨0.017 per mile (3.1x return on "investment")

üìä Loss Example

Buy miles at ‚Ç¨0.016 CPM (80% bonus promo)

Redeem for short-haul Economy at ‚Ç¨0.008 value

Loss = ‚Ç¨0.008 per mile (50% loss)

üí° Decision Rule: Before acquiring miles at a given CPM, ask yourself: "Can I realistically redeem these at a higher value?" If your planned redemption gives ‚Ç¨0.01/mile while acquisition costs ‚Ç¨0.016/mile, you are better off paying cash for the flight.
