What is Flying Blue XP?

Last updated: January 2025 · Based on current Flying Blue program rules

Flying Blue XP (Experience Points) determine your elite status from Explorer to Platinum. XP is earned primarily from flights and resets per personal qualification year. Ultimate is not an XP level but an additional layer above Platinum, based on UXP.

Important: Earning or spending miles has no effect on your XP or status.

Status structure

  • Explorer
  • Silver (100 XP)
  • Gold (180 XP)
  • Platinum (300 XP)

Ultimate is a separate layer available only to Platinum members who earn at least 900 UXP in a single qualification year.

Qualification year explained

  • Each member has a personal 12-month qualification year.
  • The year runs from the first day of a month to the last day of the month before.
  • Requalifying at the same level keeps the same year.
  • Leveling up early starts a new qualification year on the first day of the next month.

XP mechanics

XP is accumulated during your qualification year. When you cross a status threshold, the required XP is deducted and the remainder carries forward.

UXP and Ultimate

UXP is earned only on KLM and Air France flights and SAF contributions. Every UXP also counts as XP, but not every XP counts as UXP.

  • UXP follows the same qualification year as XP.
  • Maximum 1,800 UXP per year.
  • 900 UXP required for Ultimate.
  • Up to 900 UXP can roll over.

End of year and rollover

At the end of your qualification year:

  • Platinum requires 300 XP to requalify.
  • Up to 300 XP can roll over.
  • Soft landing applies if thresholds are not met.
Practical insight: XP determines your level, UXP determines Ultimate. Treat them as two related but separate counters.

What happens if you do not requalify

If you miss your XP threshold, you drop by one level only. Ultimate members always soft-land to Platinum first.

This page reflects current Flying Blue program mechanics. Always verify details in your Flying Blue account.
