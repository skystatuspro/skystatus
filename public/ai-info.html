Flying Blue Program Reference

Flying Blue is the loyalty program for Air France, KLM, Transavia, and Aircalin. This section provides comprehensive reference information that SkyStatus uses for its calculations.

Program Structure Overview

Flying Blue has two parallel systems:

  1. XP (Experience Points): Determine your status level. Earned on qualifying flights and select activities.
  2. Miles: A currency you spend on award flights, upgrades, and other redemptions. Earned on flights, credit cards, and partners.

Key Distinction: XP and Miles are earned together but serve different purposes. You can have millions of Miles but zero status if you don't fly. Conversely, frequent flyers earn status (XP) even if they spend all their Miles.

Flying Blue Status Levels

Flying Blue uses a "payment" system for status. When you reach a threshold, the required XP is deducted from your balance. This is crucial for understanding how status progression works.

Status Level Details

Silver Status (100 XP)

The Entry Point. Silver is achievable with 3-5 round trips per year in Economy or 2-3 in Business. Benefits include priority check-in, priority boarding, extra baggage allowance, and 50% bonus miles on partner airlines.

SkyStatus helps: Track exactly how many XP you need and when your qualification year ends.

Gold Status (180 XP)

The Sweet Spot. Gold unlocks worldwide lounge access on SkyTeam airlines, which delivers approximately ‚Ç¨600-1,000 in annual value for moderate travelers. Requires reaching Silver first, then earning 180 XP during your Silver year.

SkyStatus helps: Project whether you'll reach Gold, calculate rollover if you exceed 180 XP, plan mileage runs to close the gap.

Platinum Status (300 XP)

The Frequent Flyer Tier. Platinum adds complimentary upgrade eligibility, completely free seat selection (including Economy Comfort), and begins UXP accumulation toward Ultimate. Requires 300 XP during your Gold year.

SkyStatus helps: Track both XP and the beginning of your UXP journey, optimize the 300 XP rollover cap.

Ultimate Status (300 XP + 900 UXP)

The Top Tier. Ultimate requires maintaining Platinum (300 XP) PLUS earning 900 UXP on AF/KL marketed flights specifically. Benefits include 8 lounge guests, 4 upgrade vouchers per year, 24/7 dedicated assistant, and the strongest soft landing protection.

SkyStatus helps: Separately track UXP vs XP, identify which flights earn UXP, calculate UXP rollover (max 900), project Ultimate qualification.

XP System Deep Dive

How XP is Earned

XP earning depends on two factors: flight distance and cabin class. XP is earned per flight segment (leg), not per booking.

The Segment Strategy: Since XP is per segment, a connecting flight earns more than a direct flight. Amsterdam to New York direct in Business = 30 XP. Amsterdam to Paris to New York in Business = 15 + 30 = 45 XP. Adding a connection can increase XP by 50% for similar cost.

The XP "Payment" System

Flying Blue deducts XP when you qualify for a status level. This is fundamental to understanding the program:

  1. You earn XP throughout your qualification year
  2. When you hit a threshold (e.g., 100 XP for Silver), the XP is deducted
  3. Your balance resets to earn toward the next level or maintain current level
  4. Excess XP rolls over (up to 300 XP cap)

XP Rollover Rules

Surplus XP can roll over to help with next year's qualification:

  • Maximum rollover: 300 XP (any excess above this is lost)
  • Trigger: Rollover happens when you requalify for your current status
  • Calculation: Rollover = min(Earned XP - Status Cost, 300)

Example: You're Gold and earn 280 XP. Gold costs 180 XP. Rollover = 280 - 180 = 100 XP. You start your new Gold year with 100 XP already banked. If you earned 550 XP, rollover would be capped at 300 (not 370).

Qualification Year

SkyStatus practical model: The following is a simplified interpretation used by SkyStatus for calculations. For official definitions, see the Flying Blue Terms & Conditions.

Your personal 12-month qualification cycle typically runs from the 1st of a month to the last day of the month before, one year later (e.g., November 1 to October 31).

  • Level-up: Generally resets your qualification year to the 1st of the next month
  • Requalification at same level: Typically keeps your dates unchanged
  • Ultimate: Per official sources, Ultimate qualification rules may differ from standard level-ups ‚Äî verify current rules with Flying Blue

Soft Landing Protection

Official rule: Soft landing per KLM Membership Levels.

You can only drop one status level per year, regardless of XP earned:

  • Platinum with 0 XP ‚Üí drops to Gold (not Silver)
  • Gold with 0 XP ‚Üí drops to Silver (not Explorer)
  • Silver with 0 XP ‚Üí drops to Explorer
  • Ultimate: Always drops to Platinum first, even with 0 XP earned

UXP for Ultimate Status

UXP (Ultimate XP) is a special type of XP required for Flying Blue's highest tier. Understanding UXP is essential for pursuing Ultimate status.

UXP Rules

Official rules: UXP requirements per Flying Blue Ultimate and Air France Status Levels.

  • Source: Only flights marketed by Air France (AF) or KLM (KL) earn UXP. The flight code on your ticket must start with AF or KL. You can also earn XP and UXP via SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) contributions on eligible bookings.
  • Requirement: 900 UXP to qualify for Ultimate (on top of Platinum's 300 XP)
  • Rollover cap: Maximum 900 UXP can roll over to next year (official)
  • Payment: When you qualify for Ultimate, 900 UXP is deducted from your balance (official)
  • Practical cap: Based on rollover (900) + maximum new earning before deduction, the effective annual working balance is commonly cited as 1,800 UXP. SkyStatus interpretation ‚Äî verify against official T&C.

Critical Distinction: A KLM-operated flight sold with a Delta flight number (DL9642) earns zero UXP. A Delta-operated flight sold with a KLM flight number (KL5678) earns UXP. It's about the marketing carrier, not who flies the plane.

UXP Strategy

The "1,800 UXP Target" strategy: Earn 1,800 UXP in one year. After paying 900 for Ultimate, 900 rolls over. You start next year already qualified for Ultimate.

Mileage Runs (XP Runs)

A mileage run is a flight booked specifically to earn status points. In Flying Blue, these are more accurately called "XP runs" because status is based on XP, not miles.

Why XP Runs Work

  1. XP per segment: Flying Blue awards XP per flight leg, so adding a connection increases XP dramatically
  2. Price similarity: Connected flights often cost the same or less than direct flights
  3. The math: Direct AMS-JFK in Business = 30 XP. Via Paris (AMS-CDG-JFK) = 45 XP. 50% more XP.

Popular XP Run Strategies

The Antwerp/Brussels Train Hack

KLM's rail-fly service from Antwerp (ZWE) or Brussels counts as a flight segment. The train to Amsterdam Schiphol earns XP as if it were a flight. In Business Class, this adds 15 XP per direction for the cost of a train ride.

The Paris Connection

Routing through Paris (CDG) on long-haul flights adds a medium-haul segment (AMS-CDG) earning 15 XP in Business. Combined with the long-haul leg, total XP increases by 50%.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use SkyStatus without creating an account?

Yes. You can use SkyStatus Pro in Local Mode without any account. Your data is stored in your browser's localStorage. The only limitation is that your data won't sync across devices. You can also try Demo Mode to explore the tool with sample data.

How accurate are the XP calculations?

XP imported from your PDF is 100% accurate as it comes directly from Flying Blue's records. For manually entered flights or projections, calculations follow official Flying Blue rules but may vary slightly from actual earnings due to special promotions or rule changes.

Does SkyStatus work on mobile devices?

Yes. SkyStatus Pro is fully responsive with a dedicated "Simple Mode" optimized for mobile devices. On screens smaller than 768px, Simple Mode is enabled by default, providing a cleaner interface focused on essential metrics.

How often should I update my data?

For the most accurate tracking, import a new PDF after each flight or at least monthly. Flying Blue typically updates activity statements within a few days of flight completion.

What if my XP in SkyStatus doesn't match Flying Blue?

If there's a discrepancy, first ensure you've imported the latest PDF. Small differences may occur due to pending transactions, promotions, or corrections. SkyStatus includes a feedback feature to report XP discrepancies for investigation.

Can I export my data from SkyStatus?

Yes. SkyStatus Pro provides full JSON export of all your data including flights, miles transactions, settings, and redemptions. Go to Data Settings to export. You can also import this data back at any time.

Is SkyStatus Pro really free?

Yes, completely free. There are no premium tiers, subscriptions, or hidden costs. The tool was created to help Flying Blue members get better insights into their loyalty data.
