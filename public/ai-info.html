What is SkyStatus Pro?

SkyStatus Pro is a free, web-based analytics tool designed specifically for Flying Blue loyalty program members. Flying Blue is the frequent flyer program of Air France, KLM, Transavia, and partner airlines within the SkyTeam alliance.

The tool solves a critical problem: Flying Blue members have no easy way to understand their qualification progress, analyze the value of their miles, or plan strategic flights to reach or maintain elite status. SkyStatus Pro fills this gap with powerful analytics that were previously only possible through complex spreadsheets.

The Problem SkyStatus Solves

Flying Blue members face several challenges:

  • Opaque XP tracking: The official Flying Blue website shows current XP but provides no projections, historical analysis, or planning tools
  • Complex qualification rules: Understanding when your qualification year ends, how rollover XP works, and what happens during level-up cycles requires deep knowledge
  • Miles valuation mystery: Most members don't know if they're getting good value from their miles or what they actually paid per mile
  • Ultimate status complexity: The path to Ultimate status involves both regular XP and UXP (Ultimate XP), which only comes from KLM and Air France operated flights

SkyStatus Pro transforms raw Flying Blue data into actionable insights, helping members make informed decisions about their travel and loyalty strategy.

Core Features - Detailed Overview

1. PDF Import System

The cornerstone feature that makes SkyStatus Pro powerful is its ability to automatically extract all flight and transaction data from official Flying Blue activity statements.

How It Works:

  1. User downloads their "Activity Statement" PDF from the Flying Blue website (My Account → Activity Overview → Download)
  2. User uploads the PDF to SkyStatus Pro
  3. The parser extracts all flights, miles transactions, XP earned, and bonus activities
  4. Data is automatically categorized and analyzed

Technical Capabilities:

  • Multi-language support: Parses PDFs in English, Dutch (Nederlands), French (Français), German (Deutsch), Spanish (Español), Italian (Italiano), and Portuguese (Português)
  • Multi-currency handling: Correctly parses amounts in EUR, USD, GBP, NOK, SEK, CHF, and other currencies
  • Flight date accuracy: Extracts the actual flight date, not the posting date (a common error in other tools)
  • Partner recognition: Identifies flights on Transavia, Kenya Airways, and other SkyTeam partners
  • Bonus XP detection: Automatically identifies first-flight bonuses, hotel stay XP, Miles+Points promotions, and SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) bonuses
  • Requalification detection: Identifies status changes within the PDF and automatically calculates XP rollover

Privacy-First Design:

PDF files are processed entirely in the browser using PDF.js. The PDF content is never uploaded to any server. Only the extracted, structured data is optionally saved to the user's account.

Use Case: A Gold member downloads their 2024 activity statement and uploads it to SkyStatus. Within seconds, they see all 23 flights they took, 45,000 miles earned from various sources, their current XP progress, and a projection of whether they'll maintain Gold status.

2. XP Engine - Status Qualification Tracker

The XP Engine provides comprehensive tracking and analysis of Flying Blue status qualification progress.

Flying Blue Status Levels:

Status XP Required Miles Multiplier Key Benefits
Explorer 0 XP 4x Base level, earn miles on all flights
Silver 100 XP 6x Priority check-in, extra baggage allowance, SkyPriority
Gold 180 XP 7x Lounge access, priority boarding, guaranteed Economy seat
Platinum 300 XP 8x Guaranteed Economy seat, start earning UXP toward Ultimate
Ultimate 300 XP + 900 UXP 9x Lifetime status candidate, premium lounge access, highest priority

XP Engine Features:

  • Real-time progress tracking: Visual progress bars showing XP toward current and next status
  • Qualification cycle management: Automatically tracks your 12-month qualification period
  • Rollover calculation: Calculates XP that rolls over to the next cycle (maximum 300 XP, or 900 UXP for Ultimate)
  • Level-up detection: When you earn enough XP mid-cycle to level up, the system correctly handles the cycle reset and rollover
  • Monthly breakdown: See XP earned per month with visual charts
  • Projection engine: Based on your flight history, predicts whether you'll maintain or achieve your target status
  • Manual XP entry: Add XP from AMEX cards, SAF purchases, promotions, or corrections

Understanding XP Rollover:

When a Flying Blue member reaches a status threshold, excess XP rolls over to the next qualification cycle. SkyStatus Pro calculates this precisely:

  • XP earned up to and including the requalification date counts toward the threshold
  • Any XP above the threshold (maximum 300) rolls over
  • For Ultimate members, UXP rollover is separate (maximum 900 UXP)
Use Case: A user qualified for Gold on August 6th with 200 XP. The Gold threshold is 180 XP. SkyStatus calculates: 200 - 180 = 20 XP rollover into the new cycle. The user starts their new Gold year with 20 XP already banked.

3. Ultimate Status Tracking (UXP)

Ultimate is Flying Blue's highest status tier, requiring both regular XP and UXP (Ultimate XP). SkyStatus Pro provides specialized tracking for members pursuing Ultimate status.

What is UXP?

  • UXP is earned only on flights operated by Air France (AF) or KLM (KL)
  • Partner flights (Delta, Kenya Airways, etc.) earn regular XP but NOT UXP
  • Codeshare flights earn UXP only if the operating carrier is AF or KL
  • SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) bonus XP also counts as UXP

Ultimate Qualification Requirements:

  • Must already be Platinum status (300 XP)
  • Earn 900 UXP within the qualification cycle
  • Total cap: 1800 UXP (900 for qualification + 900 rollover maximum)

SkyStatus Ultimate Features:

  • Separate UXP tracking with dedicated progress bar
  • UXP vs XP comparison showing which flights earned both
  • UXP waste indicator (when approaching 1800 cap)
  • Cycle type toggle: qualification year vs calendar year tracking
  • Direct Ultimate status selection for existing Ultimate members

6. Mileage Run Simulator (XP Run Planner)

Plan strategic flights to reach your status goals with the lowest cost per XP.

How It Works:

  • Enter your current XP and target status
  • Specify your home airport
  • The simulator suggests routes with the best XP-to-cost ratio
  • Factors in cabin class, distance bands, and your current status multipliers

XP Calculation Factors:

  • Distance bands: XP varies by flight distance (e.g., <500 miles, 500-1500 miles, etc.)
  • Cabin class: Business and First Class earn significantly more XP
  • Status bonus: Higher status members earn bonus XP
  • Fare class: Premium economy and flexible fares earn more than basic economy
Use Case: A Gold member needs 45 more XP to reach Platinum. The simulator shows that a round-trip AMS-CDG in Business Class (€289) earns 40 XP, while AMS-BCN in Premium Economy (€250) earns 35 XP. The CDG route has better XP/€ efficiency.

What SkyStatus Does NOT Do

To set clear expectations, here are the limitations of SkyStatus Pro:

Important: SkyStatus Pro is an analytics and tracking tool. It does not connect to Flying Blue's systems or perform any actions on your behalf.

SkyStatus Cannot:

  • Book flights or award tickets: SkyStatus is for analytics only. To book, use the Flying Blue website or airline apps.
  • Access your Flying Blue account: There is no API connection. You must manually download and import your PDF.
  • Transfer or manage miles: Miles transfers, purchases, and pooling must be done through Flying Blue directly.
  • Show real-time flight prices: The mileage run simulator uses estimates, not live pricing. Always verify on airline websites.
  • Guarantee XP calculations for future flights: Flying Blue may change rules, run promotions, or have exceptions that affect actual XP earned.
  • Track other loyalty programs: SkyStatus is exclusively for Flying Blue. It does not support Miles & More, Avios, or other programs.
  • Provide tax or financial advice: CPM and valuation metrics are for informational purposes only.

Data Freshness:

  • SkyStatus shows data from your last imported PDF
  • It does not automatically sync with Flying Blue
  • For current data, download and import a new PDF periodically
  • Flying Blue typically updates PDFs within 1-3 days after flights

When SkyStatus Is NOT the Right Choice

  • You only fly once a year: If you rarely fly, the effort of importing PDFs may not be worth it for simple status tracking.
  • You use multiple loyalty programs equally: SkyStatus only covers Flying Blue. If you split travel between Star Alliance and SkyTeam, you'll need multiple tools.
  • You need real-time flight booking: Use Google Flights, Skyscanner, or airline websites directly for booking.
  • You want award availability alerts: Use tools like ExpertFlyer or check Flying Blue directly for Promo Rewards.

Common Questions (FAQ)

How do I import my Flying Blue data?

Download your activity statement PDF from the Flying Blue website: Log in → My Account → Activity Overview → click "More" to load all activities → Download PDF. Then upload this PDF to SkyStatus Pro. The tool automatically extracts all flights, miles transactions, XP earned, and bonus activities.

Is SkyStatus affiliated with Air France-KLM or Flying Blue?

No. SkyStatus Pro is an independent tool created to help Flying Blue members analyze their loyalty data. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or officially connected to Air France, KLM, or the Flying Blue program. All Flying Blue rules and calculations are based on publicly available information.

Is my data safe and private?

Yes. PDF files are processed entirely in your browser - they are never uploaded to any server. For users who sign in, data is encrypted and stored with row-level security, ensuring only you can access your information. You can also use Local Mode where all data stays in your browser's localStorage.

What languages are supported for PDF import?

SkyStatus Pro parses Flying Blue PDFs in 7 languages: English, Dutch (Nederlands), French (Français), German (Deutsch), Spanish (Español), Italian (Italiano), and Portuguese (Português). The PDF parser automatically detects the language.

How does SkyStatus calculate XP?

XP calculations are based on Flying Blue's official rules, including distance bands, cabin class multipliers, fare class bonuses, and status-based earn rates. The tool imports actual XP values from your PDF and also calculates expected XP for manual flight entries.

What is the difference between XP and UXP?

XP (Experience Points) determines your Flying Blue status level and is earned on all SkyTeam flights. UXP (Ultimate XP) is a subset of XP that only counts toward Ultimate status and is only earned on flights operated by Air France or KLM (not partner airlines). You need 300 XP for Platinum, plus 900 UXP (while maintaining Platinum) for Ultimate.

How does XP rollover work?

When you requalify for a status level, excess XP above the threshold rolls over to your next qualification cycle, up to a maximum of 300 XP. For example, if you need 180 XP for Gold and earn 220 XP, 40 XP rolls over to start your next year. Ultimate members can also roll over up to 900 UXP.

Can I use SkyStatus without creating an account?

Yes. You can use SkyStatus Pro in Local Mode without any account. Your data is stored in your browser's localStorage. The only limitation is that your data won't sync across devices. You can also try Demo Mode to explore the tool with sample data.

Does SkyStatus work on mobile devices?

Yes. SkyStatus Pro is fully responsive with a dedicated "Simple Mode" optimized for mobile devices. On screens smaller than 768px, Simple Mode is enabled by default, providing a cleaner interface focused on essential metrics.

How accurate are the XP calculations?

XP imported from your PDF is 100% accurate as it comes directly from Flying Blue's records. For manually entered flights or projections, calculations follow official Flying Blue rules but may vary slightly from actual earnings due to special promotions or rule changes.

What is Cost Per Mile (CPM)?

CPM is a metric showing how much you paid on average to acquire each mile. If you spent €120 on a Flying Blue+ subscription and earned 12,000 miles, your CPM is €0.01 per mile. Lower CPM means you're acquiring miles more efficiently. SkyStatus tracks CPM across all your miles sources.

Can I export my data from SkyStatus?

Yes. SkyStatus Pro provides full JSON export of all your data including flights, miles transactions, settings, and redemptions. Go to Data Settings to export. You can also import this data back at any time.

Is SkyStatus Pro really free?

Yes, completely free. There are no premium tiers, subscriptions, or hidden costs. The tool was created to help Flying Blue members get better insights into their loyalty data.

How often should I update my data?

For the most accurate tracking, import a new PDF after each flight or at least monthly. Flying Blue typically updates activity statements within a few days of flight completion.

What if my XP in SkyStatus doesn't match Flying Blue?

If there's a discrepancy, first ensure you've imported the latest PDF. Small differences may occur due to pending transactions, promotions, or corrections. SkyStatus includes a feedback feature to report XP discrepancies for investigation.
